Preliminary Studies with HLHS Chick Embryonic Model

The primary goal of this work is to formally prove the feasibility of biomechanics-origin of HLHS congenital malformations, by charting how changes in biomechanics and discovering the subsequent mechanobiological mechanisms in the Left-Atrial-Ligation (LAL) chick embryonic model of Hypoplastic Left Heart Syndrome (HLHS). Here, we demonstrate details of some of the preliminary work we have conducted.

4D Ultrasound Imaging of Chick Embryonic Heart

In ultrasound images of small animal embryos, there is a lack of contrast between blood and tissue spaces, we use compounding of multiple cardiac cycles' images to one cycle via pixel variance to differential between tissue and blood spaces. This way, we could reconstruct the 4D dynamic motions of the embryonic heart. We also utilize an automatic cardiac estimation algorithm to track cardiac motion.

Single Cell RNA-Sequencing of LAL vs normal Chick Embryonic Hearts

Preliminary single-cell RNA sequencing of chick embryonic heart ventricles for normal and LAL embryos (at ED6.5 days, HH31) was performed at the Genomic Institute of Singapore, using the Fluidigm C1 system and the Illumina Hiseq 2500 system. Quality control and normalization for different day of experiment, plate used, and cell cycle phases was performed. Cell clustering analysis together with amiGO web tool was used to identify the cell type. Out of 392 cells (roughly 50%-50% normal vs LAL), 130 were endothelial cells, 171 were myocardial cells, 59 were fibroblasts, and the rest were "others". Next, Suerat algorithm was used to return a list of differentially expressed genes, and only gene with >log2(1.0) fold change is used for subsequent analysis.

The spreadsheet of the end result can be downloaded here

Based on these results, we identified possible mechanobiological pathways that could be important for biomechanics-origin of the hypoplastic left heart, as summarized in this figure. We believe that VEGFR flow mechanosensing, focal adhesion tissue strain mechanosensing, calcium signaling, and endothelium-myocardium growth factor signaling to be important, and propose these to be tested further.
